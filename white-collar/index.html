The Types of White-Collar Crimes We Handle

White-collar crimes generally involve non-violent acts of deception, often leaving behind intricate paper trails that investigators use as evidence against the accused. These offenses, typically committed in professional or corporate settings, are known as "white-collar" due to the involvement of business professionals or public figures. Defending against these charges demands meticulous legal strategy and a thorough understanding of federal law.

Marc Kohnen provides aggressive defense for individuals facing charges in a range of white-collar crimes, including:

  • Fraud: insurance, securities, mortgage, and bank fraud
  • Embezzlement: misappropriation of funds from employers or clients
  • Extortion: use of threats for monetary or personal gain
  • Insider trading: illegal trading based on non-public information
  • Money laundering: concealing the origins of illegally obtained funds
  • Mail and wire fraud: fraud conducted through electronic or mail communications
  • Identity theft: unauthorized use of someone's personal information for financial gain

Each of these charges carries significant potential consequences. With experienced defense, however, the chances of reducing or dismissing charges improve considerably.
