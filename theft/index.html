Common Types of Theft Crimes

In San Diego, theft crimes encompass a wide range of offenses, including:

  • Petty Theft: Stealing property valued under $950.
  • Grand Theft: Taking property valued at $950 or more.
  • Shoplifting: Entering a business during regular hours with the intent to commit theft.
  • Robbery: Taking property directly from a person using force or fear.
  • White-Collar Theft: Crimes like embezzlement or fraud involving financial misconduct.
  • Extortion: Using threats to obtain money or property unlawfully.

It's important to note that burglary, while often associated with theft, involves unlawful entry into a property with intent to commit a crime and is treated as a separate offense.

Understanding Theft Crimes in San Diego

Theft is defined as the act of unlawfully taking another person's property with the intent to permanently deprive them of it.

Theft crimes vary in severity, but all carry serious consequences, including potential jail time, fines, and long-lasting damage to your reputation.

Facing these accusations alone can be daunting, which is why having an experienced San Diego theft defense attorney by your side is essential.

Petty Theft vs. Grand Theft – What's the Difference?

The distinction between petty theft and grand theft often hinges on the value of the property:

  • Petty Theft: Property valued at $950 or less. If the value is under $50, penalties may be reduced to a fine of up to $250.
  • Grand Theft: Property valued at over $950. Depending on the circumstances, this can be charged as a misdemeanor or felony, with penalties including significant fines and possible prison time.

Potential Penalties for Theft Crimes

Penalties for theft convictions in San Diego vary based on the nature of the crime, the value of the property, and prior criminal history. Common penalties include:

  • Petty Theft: Up to six months in jail and a fine of $1,000.
  • Receiving Stolen Property (as a misdemeanor): Up to one year in jail.
  • Grand Theft: Fines and prison time of up to three years, depending on whether it's charged as a misdemeanor or felony.

The stakes are high, which is why you need a skilled San Diego theft defense attorney to build a strong case on your behalf.

What Defense Strategies Do We Use for Theft Crimes?

Effective Defense Strategies for Theft Charges

Our legal team employs a variety of strategies to secure the best possible outcome for your case. These include:

  • Challenging Evidence: We analyze the prosecution's case to identify inconsistencies or violations of your rights during the investigation process.
  • Intent Defense: Demonstrating a lack of intent to permanently deprive the owner of their property.
  • Value Discrepancy: Proving the property's value was below the threshold for harsher charges.
  • Civil Rights Violations: Ensuring evidence obtained unlawfully cannot be used against you.
