If You’ve Just Been Arrested
The first 24 hours matter most

Whether the arrest happened downtown, in North County or anywhere in San Diego County, the same three rules apply. First, stay silent — you are required to identify yourself, but nothing else. Politely say you won’t answer questions without your attorney, and then stop talking; anything you say in the patrol car or at booking can be used against you. Second, don’t consent to searches of your phone, car or home. Third, get a lawyer before your arraignment — in California you must generally be arraigned within 48 hours, and decisions made at that first hearing (bail, protective orders, plea) shape the entire case.

The earlier Marc is involved, the more he can do. Before charges are filed he can contact the detective or the District Attorney directly — in past cases that pre-filing work has ended with no charges filed at all. If you can’t call yourself, a parent, spouse or friend can call for you: a large share of Marc’s clients start with a family member’s phone call in the middle of the night. Every case is different and no outcome can be promised — but silence, speed and an experienced San Diego defense attorney are always the right first moves.
